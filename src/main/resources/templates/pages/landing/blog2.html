North-East Regional Institute of Education (NERIE)

The North East Regional Institute of Education (NERIE) stands amidst the natural grandeur in Umiam village, about 20 km from Shillong city. Like other constituent units of the National Council of Educational Research and Training (NCERT), New Delhi, this Regional Institute of Education caters to the educational needs of the eight states of the North East — Assam, Arunachal Pradesh, Manipur, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Nagaland, Sikkim and Tripura.

The Institute continued to function as a field office for about ten years. In 2005, the post of Principal along with 24 faculty posts were advertised and filled by NCERT, New Delhi. As the Institute's human resource grew, it was shifted in May 2012 from Shillong to its permanent campus in Nongsder village, Umiam.

Objectives of the Institute

  • I
    Research — To undertake, aid, promote and coordinate research in all branches of education. The Institute carries out an average of eight research programmes per year.
  • II
    Pre-service Training — To conduct pre-service training courses in the region. A milestone was reached in 2015 when the two-year B.Ed course was launched.
  • III
    In-service Training — To organise in-service training programmes based on the educational needs of the eight states. On average, 20–25 training programmes are conducted per academic session.
  • IV
    Educational Materials — To develop educational materials and learning resources for both students and teachers. About ten such programmes are conducted every year.
  • V
    Extension Services — To organise extension services through seminars and conferences at regional and national level, making the Institute a learning space for recent trends in school and teacher education.
  • VI
    Flagship Programmes — To provide leadership in training, development and research on MoE flagship programmes including Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan, Rashtriya Madhyamik Shiksha Abhiyan and the newly launched Samagra Shiksha Abhiyan.
